1. Document Overview

Purpose: This playbook outlines a structured and practical approach to responding to a ransomware attack in an academic or simulated cybersecurity environment.

Scope: Applicable to all student lab environments, academic networks, and virtual systems used for cybersecurity training and research.

3. Ransomware Indicators

  • Files renamed or encrypted with new extensions (e.g., .crypt, .locky).
  • Presence of ransom notes or payment instructions.
  • Unusual CPU or disk usage.
  • Security tools disabled unexpectedly.
  • Outbound traffic to suspicious IPs or servers.

5. Incident Response Phases

Phase 1: Preparation

  • Maintain secure and verified backups of lab data.
  • Ensure endpoint detection and SIEM tools are active and up to date.
  • Conduct cyber hygiene and awareness training.
  • Maintain an updated incident contact list.
  • Use isolated virtual environments for malware testing.

Phase 2: Identification

  • Detect suspicious behavior through alerts, logs, or user reports.
  • Confirm ransomware indicators.
  • Record affected systems, accounts, and timestamps.
  • Classify the event as a ransomware incident.
  • Begin evidence collection (system logs, memory dumps).
  • Tools: Splunk, Wireshark, Sysinternals Suite.

Phase 3: Containment

Short-Term Actions:
  • Disconnect infected systems from the network.
  • Disable file sharing and remote desktop services.
  • Change passwords for impacted accounts.
Long-Term Actions:
  • Segment the network to prevent spread.
  • Monitor traffic for recurring encryption signatures.
  • Preserve forensic data for post-incident review.

Phase 4: Eradication

  • Identify and delete malicious files and registry keys.
  • Patch vulnerabilities and update security software.
  • Reimage or reset compromised systems.
  • Verify system health before reconnecting to the lab network.

Phase 5: Recovery

  • Restore clean backups and verify file integrity.
  • Re-enable lab services gradually.
  • Monitor for signs of re-infection.
  • Document system restoration steps for future reference.

Phase 6: Lessons Learned

  • Conduct a post-incident debrief with the student IR team.
  • Document the incident timeline, containment actions, and results.
  • Identify improvements for detection and response efficiency.
  • Update training materials and procedures.
  • Present a summary report to the instructor or cybersecurity lab coordinator.

7. Ransom Payment Policy

Policy: Ransom payments are strictly prohibited in academic or training environments. The focus should be on analysis, containment, and restoration from clean backups. Students should explore open-source decryption tools for research (e.g., NoMoreRansom.org).
